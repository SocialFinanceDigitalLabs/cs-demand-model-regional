Adjusting the forecast


The tool creates a simplified model of the future demand for placements based on the entry and exit rates of children between, in and out of care placements.

You have selected the base forecast so the time-period the tool will learn from and forecast has been set but you can change this by returning to the previous step.

In this step, you can also manually adjust the flow rates of young people entering care, exiting care and between placement types.


Why would I want to change the flow rate?


Adjusting the flow rates allows you to compare the difference between two similar scenarios and experiment. You can take account of contextual factors or help make better assumptions based on changes coming in the future. This helps the tool apply reality to the forecast. For example:

  • You may be aware of a step-down programme to get more young people from fostering into residential
  • You may know about a new policy to increase kinship placements or;
  • You may simply want to hypothesise what the demand could be if things escalate faster than we might expect as more young people move into care.
